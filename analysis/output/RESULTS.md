# Results (All Models)


## Belief updating under persuasion

In this sample, we measured belief change as the proportion of trials in which the model's yes/no answer differed from its prior response. Across persuasion conditions, flip rates ranged from 13.5% to 19.5%. The highest flip rates occurred for Majority vs credibility (19.5%, 95% CI [14.6, 25.5]%), Majority vague (alone) (19.0%, 95% CI [14.2, 25.0]%), Majority plain (alone) (17.0%, 95% CI [12.4, 22.8]%).

## Competitive strategy selection (head-to-head trials)

In this sample, **strength** was the most frequently selected strategy in pooled competitive trials (63.3%, 95% CI [59.4, 67.1]%). A chi-square test against a uniform three-way split gave χ² = 303.84, *p* = 1.052e-66. Per-matchup binomial tests and CIs are in the winner analysis table.

## Effects on factual accuracy

In this sample, persuasion conditions changed mean accuracy by +9.21 percentage points relative to the prior baseline. The largest net factual benefit was observed for Strength vs majority (net gain +14.5%), while the smallest net gain was observed for Majority plain (alone) (+1.0%).

## Confidence and calibration

Mean confidence shifted by -1.05 points on average. The prior calibration gap (confidence when correct minus confidence when incorrect) was -0.11; under persuasion conditions the mean gap was -0.20, indicating increased overconfidence on errors relative to baseline.

## Model differences

Models varied in susceptibility from qwen/qwen3.5-122b-a10b (flip rate 3.4%, rank 1 most resistant) to meta/llama-3.1-8b-instruct (flip rate 29.0%, most susceptible).

## Statistical significance

Paired McNemar tests compared each persuasion condition's accuracy against the prior; Wilcoxon signed-rank tests assessed paired confidence shifts. After Holm–Bonferroni correction, 8 Holm-corrected tests reached p<0.05.

## Summary

In this sample, persuasion altered model beliefs (mean flip rate 16.2%) with heterogeneous effects on accuracy and confidence. Competitive selection patterns and susceptibility differences are reported with uncertainty intervals in the accompanying tables.
